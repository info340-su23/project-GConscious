INFO 340 Project Proposal

Problem/Data Domain

1. Overview

The question of interest we raised as a group is how can we reinforce the government website's functionality and make it easier for everyone to access with correct information. After doing some research online with our group members, we found out that the capability to search on government websites is limited. The website of the Department of Entrepreneur Service from Washington State would be a good example for explanation: some of the information is sorted into the wrong category, some of the information on contract details are incomplete and outdated, and the website could be a challenge for some users to navigate. However, there are some existing attempts that can solve part of the problems. For instance, most of the state websites have the function to contact the department office, which would be a good way to update the information, but it takes a long time to transfer the information to the technology department. Another existing feature that most government websites have is the regular update, but the problem is the information only updates in a certain cycle so it could possibly provide misinformation for people to use.

4. Summary

Overall, state government websites are notorious for being nearly indecipherable when trying to navigate their contents. As such, navigating through government platforms can prove to be difficult in finding the exact contents you desire. This includes poor categorization and information tagging with terms that aren’t familiar to the general public. For first time business owners, finding a contract may be more of an arduous process than those who have experience. We hope to make that process much smoother and inviting.
